History of UH of Katy

Milestones

Community feedback

Rep. Bill Caligari and Sen. Glenn Hegar gather feedback from the Katy community on higher education needs. Subcommittee formed to explore two areas of interest: energy and health care

2008

Support from Texas Legislature

With critical help from Sen. Lois Kolkhorst, Sen. Joan Huffman, Rep. John Zerwas and Rep. Mike Schofield, in 2015 the Texas Legislature approved $46.8 million for UH to purchase land near intersection of Grand Parkway and I-10.

2015

Katy Task Force

UHS Vice Chancellor Paula Short seats the Katy Task Force to plan and advance the development of a new building and its educational programs.

2015

UH/HCC Engineering Academy

Through partnership with Houston Community College, the UH Cullen College of Engineering begins offering programs in Katy

2016

Groundbreaking for New Facility

UH and UHV break ground on the new Katy Academic Building that will house unique programs from both institutionsPress release >

May 2018

Open for Fall 2019

Doors opened to classes for the first time this fall. Classes include nursing, engineering, business, education, criminal justice, gaming and psychology.Press release >

August 2019

Grand Opening Ceremony

The Katy Academic Building is opened with a ribbon cutting and celebration.Press release >
