College News Briefs

A selection of faculty, staff, student and alumni happenings

October 23, 2025 —
  • Alumni
  • College News
  • Education
  • Faculty & Staff
  • Student Success
Share this story

October 2025

Three-time UH graduate Monica Gonzalez, an associate professor of mathematics education at East Carolina University, is set to release the book “Promoting Equitable Math Instruction: Exploring Elementary Teachers’ Stories” in December 2025. Gonzalez earned a Ph.D. in curriculum and instruction in 2017, an M.Ed. in curriculum and instruction in 2013, and a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2007.

September 2025

Ainsley Castillo, who earned a B.S. in human development and family sciences in 2023, won 2024-25 Rookie Teacher of the Year from Jackson Elementary School in Chapel Hill ISD. Castillo teaches pre-kindergarten at the school.  

Seven faculty members earned promotions, effective September 2025:  

  • Charlotte L. Carp to clinical associate professor (Department of Educational Leadership & Policy Studies)  
  • Bulent Dogan to clinical full professor (Department of Curriculum & Instruction)  
  • Theresa M. Fedor Amador to clinical associate professor (Department of Psychological, Health, & Learning Sciences)  
  • Detra Johnson to associate professor with tenure (Department of Educational Leadership & Policy Studies)  
  • Erica Jordan to clinical full professor (Department of Psychological, Health, & Learning Sciences)  
  • Anne Katz to clinical full professor (Department of Curriculum & Instruction)  
  • Allison Master to associate professor with tenure (Department of Psychological, Health, & Learning Sciences) 

Ashaley Jemela, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2024, won 2024-25 Rookie Teacher of the Year from James C. Neill Elementary in Fort Bend ISD. Jemela teaches second-grade reading, writing and social studies at the school. 

Samantha Davila, who earned a B.S. in human development and family sciences in 2024, won 2024-25 Rookie Teacher of the Year from Sugar Mills Elementary in Fort Bend ISD. Davila teaches first-grade math and science at the school. 

Emily Hinojosa, who earned a B.S. in human development and family sciences in 2024, won the 2024-25 District Rookie Teacher of the Year Award from Spring ISD and the Rookie Teacher of the Year from her school, Smith Elementary, where she teaches first grade.

Cierra Nickerson has been named principal at Rose M. Avalos P-TECH School in Aldine ISD. Nickerson previously served as the district’s director for professional learning. She earned an M.Ed. in administration and supervision in 2018.

April Peters-Hawkins, a professor of educational leadership, has been appointed by President Renu Khator to join the newly formed UH Faculty Council. She will represent the College of Education. 

August 2025

Higher education Ph.D. student Hope Pacheco has been named associate dean of students and executive director of the Gator Resource Center at the University of Houston-Downtown. Pacheco previously served as assistant dean of students at UH. An UH alum with a Master of Social Work, she expects to graduate with her doctorate in higher education leadership and policy studies in December 2025.

Chasity Fisher, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2021, won 2024-25 Teacher of the Year from Clear Falls High School in Clear Creek ISD. Fisher teaches health science and pharmacy at the school.

Steven Hernandez, who earned an M.Ed. in administration and supervision in 2025, won 2024-25 Teacher of the Year from Meadow Wood Elementary in Spring Branch ISD. Hernandez teaches first grade at the school. 

Doctoral alum Cashuna “Shun” Huddleston was elected to serve as president-elect of the American Psychological Association’s Division 35 (Society for the Psychology of Women) for 2025-26; she will assume the role of president the following year. Huddleston earned a earned a Ph.D. in counseling psychology in 2015 and founded New Way Psychological Services in Houston, where she currently works as a licensed psychologist. 

July 2025

Diego Benitez, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2024, won 2024-25 Rookie Teacher of the Year from Golbow Elementary in Katy ISD. Benitez teaches fifth-grade math and science at the school. 

June 2025

Leonora (Lora) Kaldaras, an assistant professor in the teaching and learning program, has been named a 2025 National Academy of Education/Spencer Postdoctoral Fellow. As part of the prestigious fellowship, Kaldaras will participate in professional development retreats and receive mentorship to support her research on AI-assisted learning.

Kregg Cuellar, who earned an Ed.D. in professional leadership in 2011, has been named Spring ISD interim superintendent as of June 2. Cuellar, who has 20 years of experience in public education, most recently served as chief of academics and school leadership for the district.

Alyssa Gutierrez, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2024, was named the 2024-25 Rising Star Teacher of the Year at Channelview ISD’s Anthony Aguirre Junior High. Gutierrez teaches seventh-grade social studies at the school.

May 2025

Savannah Moffitt, a participant in the UH Alternative Certification Program for Teachers, won Rookie Teacher of the Year from Alvin Junior High School in Alvin ISD. Moffitt teaches sixth grade science at the school.

April 2025

Two faculty members earned UH Teaching Excellence Awards for 2024-25: Clinical Professor Heather Domjan (recognized in the instructional/clinical category) and Clinical Associate Professor Anne Katz (recognized for community engagement).

March 2025

Anthony Encarnacion, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2024, won the 2025 District Rookie Teacher of the Year Award from Fort Bend ISD and the Rookie Teacher of the Year from his school, Sullivan Elementary, where he teaches special education.

February 2025

Carolina Quinones, who earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2020, won Teacher of the Year at the School of Science and Technology - The Woodlands, where she teaches fifth-grade English, language arts and reading.

Alumna and current M.Ed. student Jacqueline Martinez was awarded Teacher of the Year from Wilson Elementary in Cypress-Fairbanks ISD. Martinez, who’s pursuing a master’s degree in administration and supervision, teaches first grade at the school. She earned a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2021. 

January 2025

Alex Botelho, who earned an M.Ed. in special populations in 2024 and a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2022, has been named 2024-25 Campus Teacher of the Year at Carter Academy in Aldine ISD. Botelho, who teaches elementary music won Rookie Teacher of the Year in 2022-23. 

Salma Yousef, who earned an M.Ed. in curriculum and instruction in 2023 and a B.S. in teaching and learning in 2020, won ESL Teacher of the Year from Holley Elementary School in Fort Bend ISD. Yousef teaches third-grade reading, writing and social studies at the school.

    University of Houston Awarded $1M to Study Teacher Certification Pathways, Outcomes

    New UH Study Finds All STEM Stereotypes Are Not the Same

    New research from Assistant Professor Allison Master found that students don’t view all STEM subjects alike. Boys and girls generally believe that engineering and computer science are professions for males, while the tide has changed some for math and science — with girls slightly favored or equal.
