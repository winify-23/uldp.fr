Our Research

  2. Gerald D. Hines College of Architecture and Design

Architecture and design impact every aspect of our daily lives. The research contributions of our Hines College’s faculty and students influence communities not only locally in Houston but throughout the world. Our College supports the research process through assistance with identifying funding opportunities, preparing and submitting research proposals, and coordinating post-award administration.

Research Stories

  • RESEARCH PROFILE: Proposal Investigates the “Mesocosm”

    Daniel Jacobs awarded University of Houston New Faculty Research Grant

  • Hines College Professor Awarded SOM Foundation Research Prize

    Dalia Munenzon and research partner Liz Gálvez (Yale Architecture) explore heat resiliency strategies for vulnerable communities.

  • RESEARCH PROFILE: Proposal Excavates the Ecological Legacies of Colonial Territorial Expansionism

    Deepa Ramaswamy awarded University of Houston New Faculty Research Grant.

  • Defining a Future with Automation

    A conversation with Andrew Kudless, the College's William D. Kendalll Endowed Professor of Design Technologies, exploring AI and design education

  • Racing Toward the Future

    Industrial design students create interior and exterior EV concepts in College’s first automotive design studio sponsored by Photon Auto, TexPower EV Technologies, and IQP (Integrated Quantum Photonics).

  • Hines Professors Receive VentureWell Grant for Sustainable Design

    Assistant professor for UX/UI advanced design technology Min Kang and associate professor and co-director of Industrial Design Jeff Feng received a VentureWell Course and Program grant for their proposal, Design for Sustainable Consumption in People's Daily Living: Exploration and Entrepreneurship.

  • Hines College Researchers Develop Affordable, More Precise Prosthesis

    UH industrial design students David Edquilang (’22) and Niell Gorman (’21), along with ID co-director and professor Jeff Feng, recently received a Bronze Award from the Industrial Designers Society of America’s International Design Excellence Awards for Knack – an upper limb prosthesis. 

  • UH Researchers Engage Community to Create Award-Winning Plan for Resilience

    A plan developed to transform a former landfill in southwest Harris County into a town center, park and flood control facility has been recognized by industry peers and local government. The city of Houston proclaimed August 23, 2022, as Ruffino Hills Redevelopment Community Day, acknowledging efforts by Hines College professor Bruce Race, his students, and the community.

  • Hines College's CDRC Wins 2022 Great Places Award

    The Environmental Design Research Association (EDRA) recently selected the Hines College's Community Design Resource Center's (CDRC) Greater Northside Urban Design Toolkit as the recipient of the 2022 Place Planning Award included in this year's Great Places Awards. 

  • Grant Funded Workshop Goes "Beyond the Survey"

    Hines College assistant professor Deepa Ramaswamy organized and led a workshop to critically evaluate the teaching of architectural history survey courses. The Beyond the Survey workshop resulted as part of a grant Ramaswamy received from the Global Architectural History Teaching Collective (GAHTC) and The Andrew W. Mellon Foundation.

  • Habitat Project Receives Texas APA Resilience Gold Award

    Professor Bruce Race's habitat project, Robins Landing Town Center: Strategies and Guidance for Resilience, was awarded the Texas American Planning Association (APA) Resilience Gold Award. This award recognizes a strategy increasing the ability of a community to recover from and adapt to shocks and stresses of natural disasters, human-caused disasters, climate change, and other disasters. 

  • UH Professors Named Senior Members of National Academy of Inventors

    Industrial Design co-director and professor Jeff Feng is among six University of Houston professors that have been named Senior Members of the National Academy of Inventors (NAI), the most UH faculty members to earn the honor in a single election year.

  • Developing Strategies to Increase Access to Electric Vehicles

    With a projected significant increase of electric vehicle ownership over the next decade, Hines College professor Bruce Race is among the University of Houston researchers who are working to better understand the infrastructure necessary to support EV adoption, while broadening access to new electric mobility technologies.  

  • ID Student Awarded Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship

    UH awarded Gerald D. Hines College of Architecture and Design industrial design student Anne-Elisabeth Baker with a Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship to further her research on recycling plastic and generate innovative ways of fostering sustainability on the University’s main campus.

  • Black Hair Inspires Architecture Embodying Black Identity

    The Graham Foundation for Advanced Studies in the Fine Arts announced that University of Houston Gerald D. Hines College of Architecture and Design Professor Sheryl Tucker de Vazquez’s proposal Hair Salon: Translating Black Hair Practices for Architecture Using Computational Methods is among its 2021 grant award recipients.
