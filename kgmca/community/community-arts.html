UH Community Arts Academy

Where Passion Meets Purpose—
For All Ages, All Stages

Get in Touch

 

Homegrown Houston Artistry

The University of Houston Community Arts Academy offers eclectic, adventurous, premium-quality learning experiences in music, theater, dance, & visual arts. The Community Arts Academy is a UH home for any Houstonian, regardless of age, who wants to grow through creativity.
  • Grow more self-assured, confident, & disciplined
  • Grow more curious about the diverse world of arts & ideas
  • Grow more empathetic & collaborative
  • Grow more observant of beauty & meaning in the world
  • Grow more enthusiastic about your own potential for excellence

The UH Community Arts Academy also offers UH artists with career expanding opportunities to teach, perform for, & collaborate with Houston communities. 

Join our diverse, supportive community & enjoy the wondrous opportunities & resources the University of Houston offers growing artists.

 

Community Arts Academy

The Community Arts Academy offers personalized, year-round music lessons for students of all ages & experience levels. Our dedicated instructors provide a supportive & encouraging environment where creativity & confidence can grow.

We currently offer private lessons in piano, violin, viola, cello, organ, harp, guitar, flute, & voice— with additional instruments available upon request (subject to availability).

Our goal is to help every student become a well-rounded musician, developing both technical skills & a lifelong love of music through mentorship, collaboration, & community.

MUSIC LESSONS: Voice & Instrument Studies

Private & Preparatory Music Instruction
For students seeking personalized, ongoing, & comprehensive training in the arts, the Community Arts Academy offers high-quality instruction led by experienced teachers & professional musicians.

We currently offer lessons in piano, violin, viola, cello, organ, harp, guitar, flute, & voice, as well as programs in choral & mariachi studies. Additional instruments may be available upon request.

Our approach focuses on developing well-rounded musicians in a supportive, collaborative environment—helping students build confidence, creativity, & lifelong musical skills.

Interested in getting started?
Fill out our inquiry form HERE.

Kids & Teens enrolled in piano, violin, viola, cello, organ, harp, guitar, & voice studies at the Community Arts Academy receive two weekly classes:  

  • Weekly private lesson with CAA Teaching Fellows with guidance from UH Moores School master faculty. Students may enroll in either 30-, 45-, or 60-minute lessons.
  • Weekly Saturday group learning experiences, which include:  
    • Musicianship classes that improve music literacy, music composition skills, improvisation skills, & collaboration skills.
    • Monthly performance classes with special guest teachers & supportive feedback from peers.
    • Monthly University Adventures that nourish curiosity & passion. Destinations include KUHF public TV & radio studios, the world class UH piano repair workshop, the UH college of Architecture, the UH Creative Writing Center, & other fascinating & inspiring places. 

All actively enrolled music students will receive free tickets to all UH Moores School of Music performances. 

Adult music studies students receive a weekly private lesson, plus opportunities to participate in monthly performance salons, online music theory courses, piano chamber music experiences, plus free access to special topics seminars.

All actively enrolled music students will receive free tickets to all UH Moores School of Music performances. 

PART 1:

FOR ADULTS (16 & up). No prior experience required.

Master the fundamentals of piano playing in this fun & inspiring group music making class. Held at the UH Moores School of Music & led by a master piano teacher, this course delves into various musical styles, from classical to pop, fostering a comprehensive understanding of piano techniques.

Participants will delve into piano technique essentials, sight-reading skills, expressive playing, rhythmic precision, harmonic comprehension, & ear training.

Cost: $450 for ten hour-long group classes in the fall/spring or five 90-minute group classes in the summer 

Day & time: Mondays at 5:30 p.m. (fall 2025 classes September 15 – November 17)

Location: Moores School of Music, 3333 Cullen Blvd, Houston, TX 77004

PART 2:

FOR ADULTS (16 & up). Prerequisite for enrollment: Fundamentals of Piano Part 1 or 6-12 months of piano instruction 

This 10-week class helps beginner pianists build their skills & continue the musical explorations that began in Fundamentals of Piano Part 1.  Having already gained basic proficiency in music reading & keyboard technique, students in this class will expand their repertory of performable, beginner piano pieces in a variety of styles.  This class is designed to help beginner pianists reach new levels of musical excellence, while having fun in a supportive, social learning environment.   

Cost: $450 for ten hour-long group classes in the fall/spring or five 90-minute group classes in the summer

Day & time: Mondays at 6:30 p.m. (fall 2025 classes September 15 – November 17)

Location: Moores School of Music, 3333 Cullen Blvd, Houston, TX 77004

OR ADULTS (16 & up). No prior experience required.

Master the fundamentals of guitar playing in this fun & inspiring, ten-session group music making class. Jeremy Garcia, affiliate professor of guitar at the UH Moores School of Music, will introduce students to a variety of music styles, including classical, flamenco, jazz & American popular music, all employing finger-style methods.

Students will receive instruction in guitar technique, music reading, tone production, musical expression, rhythm, harmony, & ear-training.

For kids 5-6 years old. No prior experience required.

Does your 5- or 6-year-old love solving mysteries, using their imagination, or discovering new things? The Piano Detectives Club is a fun & engaging way for young beginners to start their musical journey! 
In this exciting weekly class, your child will: 

  • Become a "Piano Detective" & solve musical clues 
  • Learn real piano skills through playful, hands-on activities 
  • Use special tools & materials made just for young beginners 
  • Grow confidence, creativity, & a love for music! 

Taught by a caring, trained teacher, this program is thoughtfully designed to meet young children where they are—through movement, games, storytelling, & music-making. Give your child a joyful & strong start at the piano in a setting that feels more like an adventure than a lesson. 

Now enrolling – spots are limited! 

Cost: $450 for ten hour-long group classes in fall/spring 

Day & time: Mondays at 4:00 p.m. (fall classes September 15 – November 17)  

Location: Moores School of Music, 3333 Cullen Blvd, Houston, TX 77004

Frequently Asked Questions

Absolutely! Our Community Arts programs are for everyone, & most of our programs are beginner-friendly. Only our Piano Fundamentals Part 2 requires previous experience (Piano Fundamentals Part 1 or 6 months of previous piano instruction).

We offer the following discounts for students enrolling in private lessons:
  • UH Faculty/Staff/Student Discount: 10%
  • Early Registration Discount: 10% (dates vary depending upon semester, available upon request)
Available scholarships for private lessons:
  • Currently enrolled UH students can apply for the Cougar Artist Scholarship for private lessons. Subject to availability.

We offer payment plans for private lessons students in the fall & spring semesters only. Students can opt into one of several payment options:
  • Pay tuition in full before 1st lesson.
  • Pay 50% of tuition before 1st lesson & the remainder by September/February 15th.
  • Pay 50% of tuition before 1st lesson, 25% by September/February 15th, & remaining 25% by October/March 15th.
Payment plans are not available for the following:
  • Non-credit choir
  • Non-credit mariachi
  • Group classes (adult fundamentals group classes)
  • Cougar Artist Scholarship recipients

Community Arts Academy private lessons follow the UH semester schedule:
  • Fall: Begins late August (14 lessons)
  • Spring: Begins mid-January (14 lessons)
  • Summer: Flexible scheduling between June & early August (7 lessons)
Group class dates are listed in the class description.

To cancel your enrollment, please email us at caa@uh.edu as soon as possible. Cancellations made before the first lesson may be eligible for a partial refund, depending on the timing & program type. Once the semester has begun, refunds are not guaranteed & are considered on a case-by-case basis. Please refer to our full cancellation & refund policy for details.

We know parking on a busy university campus can be a bit of a puzzle, but we’ve got options to help make it easier for our Community Arts Academy families:
  • Visitor Parking: You’re welcome to park in any designated visitor sections or garages on campus. These are pay-by-hour & can be paid via credit card or Text-to-Pay (instructions are posted at each location).
  • Event Parking: For some events, parking attendants will be on-site to help direct traffic & assist with payment.
  • CAA Student Parking Packages: We offer special parking packages just for our students, which include parking validations for use during lessons & events.
Heads-up about game days: During major campus events — especially home football games — parking can be more limited, & validations may not be accepted. We’ll do our best to notify families in advance when this might affect your visit, & we recommend allowing extra time to find parking on those days.If you have questions about parking or want to learn more about our validation options, feel free to reach out to us at caa@uh.edu.

Before beginning private lessons, some students are invited to a brief Teacher Consultation Meeting. This is a relaxed, informal session — not a full lesson — where we get to know your musical background, goals, & learning style. It helps us match you with the teacher who’s the best fit for your needs.There’s no pressure to perform; it’s simply a chance to connect, ask questions, & ensure a great start to your learning experience. If a Teacher Consultation Meeting is recommended for your registration, we’ll reach out to schedule it after you sign up.
