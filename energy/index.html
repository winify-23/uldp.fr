Powering the Future

The Energy University® — where research, education, and collaboration drive reliable, affordable, and sustainable solutions.

  1. UH Home
  2. Energy

At the University of Houston, we don’t just study energy — we shape its future. Located in the heart of one of the world’s most dynamic energy hubs, we are surrounded by industry leaders, innovative startups, and forward-thinking organizations. This unique position fuels our multidisciplinary approach, bringing together experts in engineering, science, policy, and business to drive transformative solutions.

Our commitment spans research breakthroughs, cutting-edge education, and deep community partnerships. Whether improving existing systems, exploring new technologies, or shaping future talent, we are committed to tackling tomorrow's toughest energy challenges.

Increasing Energy Demand

As economies expand and technology advances, energy consumption continues to rise across sectors—from manufacturing and transportation to data centers and digital infrastructure. The University of Houston is developing and evaluating scalable energy systems that can meet this growing demand without straining existing resources or compromising reliability. Our work spans advanced energy storage, grid modernization, efficiency innovations, and diverse generation sources, including hydrocarbons, renewables, and next-generation fuels like hydrogen. The goal: a secure, flexible energy future that supports both population growth and industrial progress.

Safe Offshore Operations

Operational safety is critical, especially in the demanding environments of offshore energy production. The University of Houston leads in developing technologies and practices that enhance the safety and reliability of offshore systems, including hydrocarbon extraction platforms and subsea infrastructure. Our interdisciplinary teams tackle challenges through risk assessment, robotics, remote monitoring, and emergency response research. The result is a more resilient offshore industry that protects both workers and the environment while supporting continued energy development.

Explore Subsea Systems Institute

Reducing Emissions

Emissions affect more than just the atmosphere—they shape the quality of the air we breathe, the health of our communities, and the stability of our climate. At the University of Houston, we explore innovative pathways to reduce emissions through carbon management strategies that capture, store, and repurpose emissions from industrial and hydrocarbon-based energy processes. Our work focuses on practical, scalable approaches that minimize environmental impact while supporting continued economic and industrial growth. By reducing emissions thoughtfully and strategically, we help pave the way for a future that is both energy-rich and environmentally sound.

Explore Carbon Management

Ecological Resilience

Nature is a powerful force—and it's one of our greatest allies in building a more resilient, healthier Houston. At the University of Houston, we align energy innovations with nature-based solutions to strengthen Houston’s ability to withstand storms, heat, and other climate threats. A key part of our work is our Coastal Center—a living lab where researchers, students, and communities come together to test bold ideas and grow real-world solutions that go far beyond this crown jewel of local natural spaces.

Texas Finalizes $1.8 Billion for Microgrid Program to Boost Critical Infrastructure Resilience

June 11, 2025

Texas Finalizes $1.8 Billion for Microgrid Program to Boost Critical Infrastructure Resilience

Texas has approved $1.8 billion for a new microgrid program to boost backup power at critical sites such as hospitals, water systems, and emergency services. The Texas Backup Power Package Program will deploy on-site systems combining solar, batteries, and gas generation, offering a faster, more flexible alternative to large-scale plants. As traditional grid projects face delays, this marks a strategic shift toward distributed resilience amid rising demand and increasingly frequent extreme weather.

UH Energy Symposium Series – Congressman Robert Latta Calls for Urgency, Innovation, and Energy Security

June 2, 2025

UH Energy Symposium Series – Congressman Robert Latta Calls for Urgency, Innovation, and Energy Security

At the close of UH Energy’s Energy Transition Winds of Change symposium—co-hosted by the Center for Carbon Management in Energy (CCME)—U.S. Congressman Robert Latta delivered a keynote urging bold action on America’s energy future. Emphasizing the need for scalable energy infrastructure, domestic nuclear fuel sourcing, and streamlined regulation, Latta called for an “all-of-the-above” approach to secure, affordable, and sustainable power. His message: the U.S. must act quickly, think strategically, and listen to innovators driving the transition forward.

TIEEP Spring Energy Forum Highlights Practical Efficiency Innovations

May 21, 2025

TIEEP Spring Energy Forum Highlights Practical Efficiency Innovations

The 2025 TIEEP Spring Energy Forum, held on May 8 at the UH Tech Bridge, brought together industry leaders to spotlight practical innovations in industrial energy efficiency. Hosted by the Texas Industrial Energy Efficiency Program in partnership with UH Energy and SECO, the forum featured expert presentations on low-capital energy assessments, high-efficiency steam systems, and cutting-edge digital monitoring tools. From simple operational improvements to AI-powered diagnostics, the event emphasized scalable strategies to reduce costs, cut emissions, and boost performance across the oil refining and chemical sectors.

Find Your Energy Sector

Energy Production

Energy production is all about generating power — from hydrocarbons like oil and gas to renewables like wind, solar, hydrogen, and nuclear energy. It’s where raw resources are harnessed to meet global energy demand.
  • Our programs in petroleum engineering, mechanical engineering, geology, and chemistry equip you with the technical expertise to tackle exploration, extraction, and production challenges.
  • Graduate options like the Fundamentals of Petroleum Engineering Certificate help working professionals deepen their production-specific skills.

Energy Processing & Storage

Energy processing and storage focus on refining raw resources into usable energy and developing technologies to store it efficiently — from fuels and batteries to hydrogen.
  • Undergraduate degrees in chemistry and process safety engineering provide the scientific and technical knowledge needed to improve energy conversion and manage risks.
  • Graduate certificates in process safety engineering and energy risk management allow you to advance your career.
  • The Hydrogen Economy Program, help professionals expand their expertise in this rapidly advancing sector.

Energy Distribution & Infrastructure

Energy distribution and infrastructure are about delivering energy where it’s needed — through power grids, pipelines, fuel transport systems, and smart technologies.
  • Undergraduate programs in civil engineering, systems engineering, industrial design, and construction engineering equip you to design, build, and maintain the critical infrastructure behind energy networks.
  • Micro-credentials like Automation and Autonomy of Mobile Robots Using ROS and Robotics in Energy help professionals stay ahead of automation trends in the field.

Energy Sustainability & Environmental Solutions

Energy sustainability and environmental solutions focus on reducing environmental impact — through carbon capture, waste-to-energy innovations, and renewable integration.
  • Our graduate certificates in global climate, energy policy, and energy risk management prepare professionals to drive sustainability strategies and environmental performance.
  • Programs like the CCUS Executive Education Program and Plastics Circular Economy equip you to tackle climate challenges head-on.

Energy Policy, Markets, & Management

Energy policy, markets, and management deal with the business, regulatory, and strategic aspects of the energy industry — from policymaking and regulation to investment and risk management.
  • Our graduate certificates in energy finance, investment analysis, energy risk management, and energy policy provide specialized expertise to navigate complex market dynamics.
  • The Managing Non-Technical Risks in Energy program builds critical leadership skills to address stakeholder, governance, and reputational challenges.
