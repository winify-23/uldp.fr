Software Applications

Fertitta College of Medicine students will use the following software applications to enhance and aid their education.

MedHub

Fertitta College of Medicine learning management system containing a suite of modules for various medical education tasks. Users can log into MedHub using their CougarNet username.

Poll Everywhere

Poll Everywhere is available to students at no cost. Instructors activate a Poll Everywhere activity or survey and students scan a QR code to interact with the class activity via text messaging, a web browser, or the Poll Everywhere app.

Microsoft Teams

A workspace for real-time collaboration and communication, meetings, file and app sharing, and more. Read more about Microsoft Teams.

Boards & Beyond

An online resource for medical students and educators that makes complex concepts easy to understand, B&B can be used to supplement curriculum and is useful for CBSE and USMLE exam prep. Users can log into B&B using their UH credentials.

Aquifer

Aquifer is an application that helps students build clinical reasoning skills and supplements clinical experiences through evidence-based, peer-reviewed and continuously updated courses and virtual patient cases.

i-Human Patients by Kaplan

I-Human Patients by Kaplan is a virtual clinical simulation software that provides a measured approach for learning how to accurately and effectively --- diagnose patients. It is a cloud-based and highly interactive application designed to help students master diagnostic-reasoning and to solve globally for misdiagnosis.

Canvas

Fertitta College of Medicine utilizes Canvas to support teaching and learning, providing students with course information and content. Users can log into Canvas using their CougarNet username.

UWorld Medical

UWorld Medical provides Fertitta College of Medicine students and faculty with an exam-like software and educator resources to aid students in mastering concepts. Access is provided by email invitation primarily to students preparing for exams.

Copilot

As part of the Microsoft 365 suite, Copilot provides students, faculty and staff an AI tool to assist with tasks and organize data. (Note: Entry of University of Houston security Level 1 and 2 data into Copilot is prohibited.)

Poll Everywhere FAQs

Poll Everywhere is available to students at no cost. Students can participate in polls and class activities from a variety of devices/methods.

  • Scanning a QR code with a mobile device
  • Sending a text message with your response
  • Using the Poll Everywhere mobile app
  • Accessing the activity through any web browser
  • Scanning a QR code with a mobile device
  • Sending a text message with your response
  • Using the Poll Everywhere mobile app
  • Accessing the activity through any web browser

This flexible system ensures all students can participate regardless of their preferred device or method.

  1. The instructor activates a Poll Everywhere activity or survey and displays it to the class.
  2. A QR code appears on the screen, linked to the instructor’s unique pollev.com URL.
  3. Students scan the QR code with their phones or tablets.
  4. Scanning the code takes them directly to the active activity page, where they can respond using:
    • Text messaging
    • A web browser
    • The Poll Everywhere app

University faculty, staff and students can use Microsoft Teams.

Boards and Beyond FAQs

An online resource for medical students and educators with instructional media and USMLE-style questions. Faculty can use B&B to supplement curriculum and students find it useful for CBSE and USMLE exam preparation.
B&B is available to students and faculty at TJFFCOM. To receive an access code and register, please contact Educational Technology at comedtec@cougarnet.uh.edu.

Aquifer FAQs

Aquifer is a safe space to practice clinical decisions with deep evidence-based articles, videos, learning modules and formative assessment. It offers comprehensive coverage of national curriculum standards and authentic clinical contexts.
Aquifer is available to students and faculty at the College of Medicine. To gain access, please contact Educational Technology at comedtec@cougarnet.uh.edu.
