UH EXTEND / FLEXIBLE ONLINE

As part of our ongoing commitment to providing flexibility and affordability to students, the University of Houston offers two distinct pathways for designated online programs: UH Extend and Flexible Online. 

Both programs are taught by the same experienced faculty who lead the University of Houston’s on-campus programs and maintain the same rigorous academic standards, ensuring students receive the same high-quality education as on-campus students.  

UH Extend is a new initiative at the University of Houston that offers fully online degree and certificate programs designed to be both convenient and affordable. With flat-rate tuition, regardless of state residency, and no additional mandatory fees, UH Extend provides a pathway to a high-quality education regardless of location.  

With Flexible Online programs, students can choose from a wider range of programs, with options to attend classes in-person at the University of Houston campus or at the University of Houston Katy campus. 

What is the difference?

UH Extend

  • Fully Online: Offers fully online degree and certificate programs.
  • Affordability: All students pay flat-rate tuition with no additional fees, regardless of location or state residency.  
  • Flexibility: 100% online with no on-campus requirements (some programs may require proctored exams). 
  • Limited On-Campus Services: Students have limited access to on-campus services.

Flexible Online

  • Variety: Includes a wider range of undergraduate and graduate degrees, minors, and hybrid options.
  • Flexible: Students can choose to take courses on-campus.
  • On-Campus Services: Access to all the University’s student services including Campus Recreation and Wellness Center, Health Center, and Career Services.

UH SESSIONS

UH Sessions gives you options in pursuing your degree by providing a variety of courses to help you get ahead or stay on track. Short sessions courses are offered through a condensed schedule during the regular semester in face to face, hybrid, or online formats. Session 2-6 and mini semester courses are considered short sessions where students can complete courses in a condensed format.
