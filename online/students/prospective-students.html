PROSPECTIVE STUDENTS

Congratulations on choosing the University of Houston, a Tier-One institution! Attending online allows you to learn from anywhere, bringing the classroom to you. College is a journey filled with ups, downs, and turns. It can be challenging, but also incredibly rewarding. We're dedicated to your success from choosing a program to graduation.

As a University of Houston Online Coog, you'll have access to all the resources you need to thrive on your academic journey. Whether you need help researching a topic or staying on top of your studies, UH provides the tools you need from acceptance to graduation. Ready to learn more about becoming a student and completing one of our distance education programs?

 

Cost

A Great Value

Earn a Tier One Education from Home

An online or hybrid credit course at the University of Houston offers you the opportunity to earn a prestigious Tier One education from the comfort of your home. As an online student, you receive the same highly valued degree as on-campus students, while also saving on traditional costs like transportation, parking, and housing. Plus, the University of Houston has been featured in several Princeton Review articles, highlighting the exceptional value of a UH degree.

Estimate the Cost- Flexible Online

Undergraduate and graduate tuition and fees vary depending on your department and residency status. For students looking to take a flexible online program, you can estimate your semester costs using the University's tuition calculator pages.

Undergraduate Academic Tuition Calculator >      Graduate Academic Tuition Calculator >


Estimate the Cost- UH Extend (100% Online)

With UH Extend, you can confidently plan for college costs. We offer fully online degree and certificate programs at a flat tuition rate, free from additional mandatory fees. We recommend consulting with a college advisor for total program costs.

UH Extend students, regardless of residency, pay the same tuition rate. Undergraduate students can also benefit from the Cougar Textbook Access Program (CTAP), with the option to opt out. Students looking to take undergraduate and graduate programs through UH Extend should visit our UH Extend cost page for more information.

UH Extend Cost >


Paying for School

Our helpful and knowledgeable financial aid staff can assist you in pursuing grants, work-study opportunities, or loans. To apply for financial aid, students must complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at fafsa.gov (school code: 003652). In addition to financial aid, consider applying for scholarships. Incoming students receive over $12 million in scholarship funds.

Choose Your Academic Program

The University of Houston offers a wide variety of undergraduate, graduate, and certificate programs available online, hybrid, and sessions, options. Explore our programs to find the one that best fits your needs.

Attend Orientation or Request a Waiver

The University of Houston offers several orientations for students to register for classes, learn about available resources, and tour the campus. Undergraduate students living outside the Houston area who are completing an online degree can request an orientation
waiver by contacting our office. Graduate student orientation is recommended but not mandatory.
