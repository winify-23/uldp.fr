Master of Arts in TESOL

Home > Master of Arts in TESOL
Share :
Advanced Education for Global English Language Teaching

Today’s organisations operate in an increasingly complex environment shaped by digital transformation, global competition, rapid innovation, and constant market change. Managers and leaders are expected not only to understand business theory, but to apply strategic thinking, lead teams effectively, and make sound decisions in real-world conditions.

The Master of Arts in TESOL (Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages) is designed for educators and professionals who want to deepen their expertise in English language teaching, curriculum development, and applied educational practice.

This programme equips participants with advanced pedagogical knowledge, research-informed teaching strategies, and professional skills applicable across schools, universities, training institutions, and international education settings.

Why This MA in TESOL Matters

Unlike general education degrees, this programme focuses specifically on the theory and practice of English language teaching in real educational environments.

Participants engage with contemporary challenges in language education, such as learner diversity, technology-enhanced learning, assessment design, and curriculum innovation, while developing practical solutions that can be applied directly in the classroom.

The programme supports professionals who aim to:

  • Enhance English language teaching effectiveness and learner outcomes

  • Design and evaluate curricula for diverse learning contexts

  • Integrate technology and innovation into language instruction

  • Advance careers in teaching, training, academic coordination, or education leadership

  • Build a foundation for further academic research or doctoral study

Program Overview

The MA in TESOL focuses on advanced knowledge and professional competence in English language teaching and education.

The curriculum is structured into two integrated phases.

Phase 1 – Advanced Coursework and Professional Development

Participants complete eight coursework modules focused on TESOL theory, pedagogy, and professional practice. This phase is designed to:

  • Strengthen understanding of second language acquisition

  • Develop effective instructional strategies for diverse learners

  • Enhance curriculum design and lesson planning capability

  • Integrate educational technologies into language teaching

  • Improve assessment, evaluation, and feedback practices

  • Build reflective, ethical, and learner-centred teaching approaches

This phase ensures graduates develop strong pedagogical competence and professional confidence in English language education.

Phase 2 – Applied Project or Strategic Integration

In the final phase, participants complete one applied research project or teaching-based inquiry aligned with their educational context or professional interests.

This component enables participants to:

  • Investigate real-world challenges in English language teaching

  • Apply research-informed methods to classroom or institutional practice

  • Analyse teaching effectiveness and learner outcomes

  • Produce practical recommendations for instructional or curriculum improvement

Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the Master of Arts in TESOL will:

  • Demonstrate advanced knowledge of TESOL theory and practice

  • Design effective teaching, assessment, and curriculum materials

  • Apply reflective and research-informed approaches to instruction

  • Integrate technology to enhance language learning

  • Strengthen professional credibility in local and international education contexts

Entry Requirements

Applicants to the Master of Arts in TESOL must meet the following criteria:

  • Hold a Bachelor’s degree from a recognised institution or an equivalent qualification aligned with EQF Level 6

  • Applicants with non-standard qualifications may be considered through Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) in accordance with ULDP policies

  • Degrees from unaccredited institutions or diploma mills will not be accepted

  • Admission decisions are made following an individual review of each applicant’s academic and professional profile

English Language Requirements

Applicants whose previous education was not conducted in English are required to demonstrate English proficiency through one of the following:

  • CEFR Level B2

    or equivalent
  • IELTS 6.5

    (with Reading and Writing at 6.5 or above) or equivalent
  • TOEFL iBT 101

    or equivalent

This programme is designed for:

  • English language teachers in schools, colleges, or universities
  • Language instructors in private or international institutions
  • Teacher trainers and academic coordinators
  • Education professionals seeking TESOL specialisation
  • Graduates planning careers in international education

This MA in TESOL emphasises applied pedagogy and professional relevance.

Participants:

  • Connect theory directly to classroom practice
  • Develop teaching strategies for real learning environments
  • Engage in reflective and research-informed professional development

The programme bridges academic theory and effective teaching practice.

Graduates are prepared to work across diverse settings, including:

  • Primary, secondary, and tertiary education
  • Private language centres and training institutions
  • International schools and cross-cultural environments
  • Online and technology-enhanced teaching platforms

Graduates commonly use the qualification to:

  • Advance careers in English language teaching and training
  • Move into curriculum design or academic coordination roles
  • Improve teaching quality and learner outcomes
  • Strengthen professional standing in international education
  • Prepare for doctoral study in education or applied linguistics

Shaping Your Teaching and Academic Journey

The MA in TESOL enables participants to design a learning pathway aligned with their teaching context and career ambitions.

Through advanced coursework and applied inquiry, participants develop the expertise, confidence, and insight required to succeed in modern English language education.
