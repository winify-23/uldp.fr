Doctor of Business Administration (DBA)

Home > Doctor of Business Administration (DBA)
Share :
Research That Solves Real Business Problems

Today’s organisations operate in an environment shaped by rapid digital transformation, global competition, regulatory complexity, and constant disruption. Senior leaders are expected not only to manage change, but to create knowledge, lead innovation, and make evidence-based decisions.

The Doctor of Business Administration (DBA) is designed for experienced professionals who want to go beyond traditional management education and apply doctoral-level research directly to real industry challenges.

This programme enables candidates to transform professional experience into rigorous, actionable research that delivers measurable impact within organisations, industries, and markets.

Why This DBA Matters

Unlike traditional doctoral programs that focus primarily on theory, this DBA is built around industry-driven research.

Candidates work on real problems drawn from their own organisations or sectors—such as strategy execution, leadership transformation, digitalisation, governance, sustainability, or organisational performance—and develop research-based solutions that can be implemented in practice.

The programme supports professionals who aim to:

  • Strengthen strategic decision-making through research

  • Lead organisational transformation with evidence-based insights

  • Contribute original knowledge to their industry or profession

  • Advance careers in executive leadership, consulting, or higher education

Program Overview

The Doctor of Business Administration (DBA) program offers 17 specialized majors and focuses on applied, practice-oriented research in business administration and management.

The curriculum is structured into two key phases:

Phase 1 - Advanced Coursework and Professional Development

    Candidates undertake in-depth study across eight core modules in management and organisational studies. This phase aims to:

  • Strengthen critical and strategic thinking

  • Integrate contemporary management theory with professional practice

  • Develop advanced analytical and decision-making capabilities

Phase 2 - Doctoral Research and Thesis

    During the research phase, candidates complete an original doctoral thesis aligned with their professional context, industry, or sector of expertise.

  • Address real-world business or managerial challenges

  • Apply rigorous research methodology

  • Produce practical insights, models, or frameworks with organisational impact

Learning Outcomes

Graduates of the Doctor of Business Administration will:

  • Demonstrate doctoral-level research competence

  • Possess in-depth expertise in their chosen specialisation

  • Apply research-based solutions to complex organisational issues

  • Enhance their professional and academic standing in leadership, consulting, or teaching roles

After completing the foundational coursework, the program places strong emphasis on independent and applied research tailored to the candidate’s professional interests.

Entry Requirements

To be eligible for the Doctor of Business Administration program, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Hold a Master’s degree from a recognized institution or an equivalent qualification aligned with EQF Level 7

  • Applicants with non-standard qualifications may be considered through Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) in accordance with ULDP policies

  • Degrees from unaccredited institutions or diploma mills will not be accepted

  • Admission decisions are made following an individual review of each applicant’s academic and professional profile

English Language Requirements

Applicants whose previous education was not conducted in English are required to demonstrate English proficiency through one of the following:

  • CEFR Level B2 or equivalent

  • IELTS 6.5 (with Reading and Writing at 6.5 or above) or equivalent

  • TOEFL iBT 101 or equivalent

Tuitionfees

Tuitionfees are subject to ULDP policies and may be revised without prior notice. Scholarships may be available for eligible applicants.

  • Application Fee: €400

  • Program Fee: €12,000

  • Payment Options: 2 Installments

This Doctor of Business Administration is designed for experienced professionals who want to apply research to real organisational and industry challenges. The programme is particularly suitable for:

  • Senior managers and executives

  • Business owners and entrepreneurs

  • Consultants and industry specialists

  • Policy makers and organisational leaders

  • Academic practitioners seeking an applied doctoral pathway

Applicants typically have substantial professional experience and a strong interest in evidence-based decision-making, leadership, and organisational improvement.

This Doctor of Business Administration is distinguished by its practice-oriented research focus. Rather than pursuing purely theoretical inquiry, candidates:

  • Investigate real problems from their own organisations or industries

  • Combine academic rigour with professional relevance

  • Develop research outputs that can be implemented in practice

The programme bridges the gap between academic research and executive decision-making, ensuring that doctoral work delivers tangible value beyond academia.

The research areas and industry focus of the DBA are aligned with the candidate's professional context and may include, but are not limited to:

  • Strategic management and execution

  • Leadership and organisational transformation

  • Digital transformation and innovation

  • Corporate governance and ethics

  • Sustainability and responsible business

  • Human capital and organisational performance

  • Entrepreneurship and business development

Candidates are encouraged to pursue research that contributes original insights to their sector while addressing practical industry needs.

Career and Professional Impact

Graduates of the DBA typically use the qualification to:

  • Strengthen their position in senior leadership or executive roles

  • Advance careers in consulting, advisory, or strategic roles

  • Transition into academic or teaching positions with a practice-based doctorate

  • Establish authority and credibility as industry thought leaders

The DBA enhances both professional influence and academic standing, supporting long-term career development across industry and education.

Shaping Your Doctoral Journey

The DBA program at ULDP enables candidates to design a doctoral pathway aligned with their professional ambitions. Through structured learning, academic supervision, and applied research, candidates develop the capacity to transform professional experience into doctoral-level knowledge and impact.

Industry-Driven Research

Conduct doctoral research based on real organisational challenges, not abstract theory. Your work is grounded in your professional environment and produces practical outcomes.

Research with Real Impact

Develop frameworks, models, and strategies that can be implemented, evaluated, and scaled within organisations and industries.

Doctoral-Level Professional Advancement

Achieve the highest level of professional qualification while enhancing leadership capability, strategic influence, and academic credibility.
